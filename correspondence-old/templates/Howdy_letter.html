We are the office that makes disability decisions for the Social Security Administration. We are writing to tell you that we are reviewing disability claim.

How We Decide Eligibility For Disability Benefits

To be eligible for disability benefits, you/he/she must have a medical condition(s) that:

    {{#if child_case}}
  • Causes marked and severe functional limitations, and
  • Has lasted or is expected to last for at least 12 months in a row, or is expected to result in death
    • {{else}}
  • Keeps you/him/her from doing any type of work, and
  • Has lasted or is expected to last for at least 12 months in a row, or is expected to result in death
    • {{/if}}

What We Will Do

We will review the medical and other information we have. If we need more information to decide whether you/he/she are/is disabled, we may arrange an exam or test which we will pay for. We may also reimburse some travel expenses to the exam or test site based on a set rate.

What You Need To Do

Please respond quickly to any letters or forms that you receive from us. Let us know right away if any of the following occur while we process this claim:

  • New doctor or hospital visit
  • Additional tests, therapy, or surgery
  • Changes in dosage, addition, or discontinuation of medication(s),
    • {{#if adult_case}}
  • Begin or return to work,
    • {{/if}}
  • New conditions develop<,/li>
  • Additional current or past medical, educational, or mental health sources not listed on the application.

You must report to SSA right away any changes to address, telephone number(s) or any other personal information.

If You Have Questions

If you have any questions or wish to provide more information, please call us at the number(s) shown below {local_office_hours}. When you call or leave a message, please provide the Case ID: {case_id}, your name/your name, his/her name, and a call back number.
